    Best Practices

    Follow these guidelines for the best data collection results.

    Validated Activities

    OpenCap Monocular has been validated on the following activities:

    • Sit-to-stand
    • Walking
    • Squats

    It may work on other activities, but these have not yet been validated. Jumping is currently not supported and does not work reliably. Support for other activities will be added in the future.

    Camera Position

    • The camera should be static throughout the trial (e.g., on a tripod). It can be moved between trials.
    • See this example trial for camera setups that have worked well for us.
    • The camera should be 30-45° from front of the participant. Pure frontal and pure sagittal views work poorly.
    • For walking, we recommend recording closer to 30° from the direction of walking. Landscape mode works well to capture several gait cycles. Results degrade when the camera is too sagittal (e.g., 60°+).
    • Results will be poor if a part of the body is occluded for a long period of time.
    • Results are more sensitive to camera positioning than in the multi-camera mode. Pilot testing is essential, as is consistency between participants and visits.
    • Unlike multi-camera mode, kinematics are optimized over the entire trial duration. Aim for the participant to only be in the frame when they are <5m from the camera and recorded at 30-45°.

    Clothing

    Do not wear baggy clothes or multiple layers.

    Lighting

    Use normal lighting conditions (avoid harsh shadows or very dim environments).

    Environment

    Avoid having multiple people in the recording frame. It is okay if people are in the distant background, but do not have two people in the foreground.

    Distance

    Ensure the entire person stays in frame for the entire movement, and record with the participant less than 5 meters from the camera.
